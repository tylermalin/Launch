Mutable Protocol Parameters

Genesis supply
Two hundred (200) total Genesis Licenses. One hundred ninety-five (195) Licenses are available for external sale. Five (5) Licenses are reserved for Mālama Labs team and production use (Dallas / DFW area). Each License is represented as an NFT-HEX geographic rights object on Cardano (CIP-68) and Base (ERC-721) via cross-chain state synchronization. There is one License per geographic hex cell — a single License conveys rights on both chains, not a separate License per chain.

Hex exclusivity
Each License conveys the exclusive right to operate a Hex Node within one H3 resolution-7 hex cell. No more than one active License may be issued per hex cell globally across all chains.

MLMA allocation
One hundred twenty-five thousand (125,000) MLMA per License. Vesting: twenty-five percent (25%), being thirty-one thousand two hundred fifty (31,250) MLMA, vests at verified hardware boot upon successful deployment registration. Seventy-five percent (75%), being ninety-three thousand seven hundred fifty (93,750) MLMA, vests linearly over twelve (12) months from the hardware boot date at approximately seven thousand eight hundred thirteen (7,813) MLMA per month. The allocation does not vest at reservation — it vests at hardware boot as described in this section.

Genesis multiplier
Genesis 200 operators receive a 1.5× Genesis Multiplier (GX) applied to their validation reward formula for Year 1 only (twelve months from network emissions commencement). The Genesis Multiplier expires permanently at the end of Year 1. It is a bootstrapping incentive, not a permanent feature of the reward formula.

Reward formula
MLMA validation rewards are computed per the formula in the Whitepaper:

R = B × DQS × GM × UF × GX × PoolFactor

where B = base rate from monthly emission pool, DQS = Data Quality Score (0.0–1.0), GM = Geographic Multiplier (0.5× to 3.0×), UF = Uptime Factor (0 at <90% uptime, 1.0 at 99%, 1.1× bonus at 99.9%+), GX = Genesis Multiplier (1.5× Year 1 only, 1.0× thereafter), PoolFactor = normalization ensuring total payouts do not exceed the monthly emission pool. Rewards are competitive and relative to the active validator set, not fixed. Mālama does not guarantee any particular dollar or token return.

Emission schedule
Year 1: 9.0M MLMA (750K/month). Year 2: 25.2M MLMA (2.1M/month). Year 3: 45.0M MLMA (3.75M/month). Years 4–5: zero emissions; all operator rewards funded by protocol revenue. Emissions commence following Genesis Hex Sale audit clearance, not automatically at a calendar date.

Audit gate before emissions
MLMA validation rewards do not begin automatically at hardware boot or at a fixed calendar date. They begin following an independent audit of the Genesis Hex Sale confirming that deployed nodes are operational, compliant, and properly registered. The audit is conducted in October 2026. Nodes that do not pass the audit are notified and supported through remediation. Vesting of the 125,000 MLMA allocation is not affected by audit status — only validation rewards are withheld until compliance is confirmed.

Uptime requirement
Active operators must maintain at least 90% uptime measured over any rolling 30-day window. Nodes below 90% uptime for an epoch earn zero validation rewards (UF = 0) for that period. Nodes offline for 90 or more consecutive days without prior written notification to Mālama are subject to License suspension review under protocol rules.

Deployment window
Customer must install, power on, and register their Hex Node using the Mālama dApp within ninety (90) days of hardware delivery to the shipping address provided at checkout. The 90-day window begins at hardware delivery, not at reservation. Licenses not activated within this window, and any unvested MLMA allocation associated with them, are automatically forfeited to the Mālama protocol treasury without refund. The 90-day window may be extended in Mālama's sole discretion on written request prior to expiration, for reasons such as verified shipping delay, permitting, or force majeure.

Relocation
Hex Nodes may not be physically relocated outside the H3 hex cell claimed under the License. Relocation invalidates the historical data baseline for that cell and may result in reputation slashing or License suspension under protocol rules. Contact support before any relocation attempt.

Governance updates
Geographic Multiplier coefficients, reward pool sizing, and methodology approvals may be modified through veMLMA governance processes described in the Whitepaper. Operators holding MLMA with a valid PONO credential may participate in governance votes on these parameters.
