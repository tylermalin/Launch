3. Categories of Data Collected

We collect Personal Data proportionate to the nature of your interaction with our systems.

Identity Data

Name, username, organization, role, and where required, identity verification documentation.

Contact Data

Email address, phone number, billing/shipping address, and communication records.

Financial and Transaction Data

Purchase records, payment metadata (processed via third parties), wallet addresses, and blockchain transaction identifiers.

Technical Data

IP address, device identifiers, operating system, browser type, session logs, and diagnostic data.

Profile Data

Account credentials, preferences, node ownership status, participation history, and support interactions.

Usage Data

Behavioral interaction data such as page views, clicks, feature usage, and session analytics.

Node and Infrastructure Data

  • Node registration and hardware identifiers
  • Deployment metadata
  • Telemetry and uptime data
  • Environmental measurement data
  • Geolocation (precise or approximate depending on configuration)
  • Signed sensor outputs and verification data
Important — geolocation is published on-chain by design. By design of the protocol, the H3 hex cell associated with each Hex Node License is published to the Base and/or Cardano blockchain. This is necessary to enforce one-hex-per-License exclusivity and to anchor signed sensor readings to their claimed location. Because blockchain records are immutable and globally readable, the hex-cell association cannot be deleted, reversed, or restricted after it is recorded on-chain, even in response to a data-subject deletion request. If you do not wish the approximate geographic area of your node to be public, do not operate a Hex Node.

Blockchain Data

  • Public wallet addresses
  • Smart contract interactions
  • Token holdings or reward eligibility indicators
  • On-chain transaction history
Important: Blockchain data is inherently public and immutable. Mālama does not control third-party access to such data.

Aggregated Data

We may generate anonymized datasets for system optimization, research, and market intelligence.
